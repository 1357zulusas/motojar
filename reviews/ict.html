Verdict

A moped-style commuter with real pace — 49 mph claimed on the 60V drivetrain, and up to 100 miles of range. Racks and a real seat make it the practical one in this garage.

Motor & speed

Power comes from a 4,000W peak brushless motor putting down 170 Nm of torque. Top speed is 50 mph (80 km/h). Ride modes: Electric / Bicycle / Pedal-assist.

Battery & range

It carries a 60V 25Ah lithium-ion battery. Manufacturer-rated range is 75–100 miles (120–160 km) — expect less if you ride it hard, uphill, or in the cold. A full charge takes 8–10 hours (60V 3.0A charger).

Build, suspension & brakes

The bike weighs 103 lbs (46.7 kg). It rolls on 20"×4.0" fat tires. Max rider load is 330 lbs (150 kg). Water resistance is rated IPX5.

Who it's for

Fit: Riders 5'2"–6'6" (158–200 cm). Seat height is 33.9 in (860 mm). Best for: daily commuting. It is street legal.

Pros & Cons

  • Street legal commuter
  • Racks and a real seat for errands
  • Surprising ~40 mph claimed pace
  • Not built for dirt
  • Middling suspension
