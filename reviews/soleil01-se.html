Verdict

The hot-rodded Soleil01 with better suspension and brakes over the base bike. This is the one to get if you actually plan to jump it.

Motor & speed

Power comes from a 3,000W peak brushless mid-drive motor putting down 220 Nm of torque. Top speed is 37 mph. Ride modes: Three-speed with zero start.

Battery & range

It carries a 48V 25Ah lithium-ion battery. Manufacturer-rated range is 45 miles — expect less if you ride it hard, uphill, or in the cold. A full charge takes 7–8 hours (54.6V 2.5A charger).

Build, suspension & brakes

The bike weighs 105 lbs (48 kg). The frame is aluminium alloy. Suspension: 40mm hydraulic fork, 6 in travel front; nitrogen shock rear. Braking is handled by hydraulic heavy-duty brakes. It rolls on 14"×2.5" front, 12"×3.0" rear Junkai tires. Max rider load is 265 lbs (120 kg). Water resistance is rated IPX6.

Who it's for

Fit: Riders 3'11"–6'1" (120–185 cm). Seat height is 29.5 in (75 cm). Best for: upgraded mini dirt. It is not street legal — ride it on private land or a track.

Pros & Cons

  • Best suspension of the Soleil family
  • Stronger brakes for real jumps
  • Feels the most 'complete'
  • $300 over the base for the same motor
  • Still a 48V bike at heart
