Verdict

Full-size frame, 2.4 kW and dual 48V batteries for 51 miles. A friendly full-size that won't snap your arms off — ideal first big bike.

Motor & speed

Power comes from a 2,400W rear gear hub (48V 30A controller) motor putting down 106 Nm of torque. Top speed is 28–37 mph.

Battery & range

It carries a Dual removable 48V 18.2Ah lithium, UL 2849 compliant battery. Manufacturer-rated range is 68 miles throttle / 155 miles pedal assist — expect less if you ride it hard, uphill, or in the cold. A full charge takes 9–10 hours (two chargers included).

Build, suspension & brakes

The bike weighs 116 lbs. Suspension: Inverted aluminum front fork; rear shock. Braking is handled by front and rear hydraulic brakes + electronic brake. It rolls on 25"×3" dirt bike tires (70/100-19) on 7074 alloy rims. Max rider load is 286 lbs. Water resistance is rated IPX4.

Who it's for

Fit: Adults. Best for: full-size on a budget. It is not street legal — ride it on private land or a track.

Pros & Cons

  • Full-size frame on a budget
  • Dual batteries as standard
  • Friendly, predictable power
  • 37 mph is modest for the size
  • 2.4 kW won't thrill experienced riders
